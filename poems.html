A Long Way Back

As I walk along the road,
The only footsteps in the snow,
I gaze up at the towering trees,
and shuffle forward with relative ease.

I pass on by a babbling brook,
a fallen log and a cave-like nook.
The snow falls lightly, soft and free,
The wind blows faintly a gentle breeze.

The sun concludes its daily descent,
The breeze brings forth an enchanting scent.
A smile creeps across my face,
Newly invigorated I pick up the pace.

In the distance and growing near,
Quiet, serene, provoking a tear,
A familiar visage approaches fast,
I am finally here, home at last.

A Constant Battle

Days grow long and never end,
Nights continue the dreary trend.
Lethargy and apathy rule the day,
A bleak illusion guides the way.

The melancholy seeps down deep,
A sense of dread dismisses sleep.
Among the shadows emotions swarm,
Dark and heavy like a raging storm.

All I wish is for a mind at peace,
For the doubt and wariness to simply cease.
Alas, I find no simple cure,
Only a future adrift and obscure.

My Daydreams

I'd like to sail across the sea,
I'd like to plant a sturdy tree.
I'd like to bask in the morning sun,
I'd like to run and race for fun.

I'd like to gaze upon the stars,
I'd like to leave and avoid all cars.
I'd like to sit and read a book,
I'd like to ponder by a babbling brook.

I'd like to explore a brand-new world,
I'd like to jump and dance and twirl.
I'd like to play an exciting game,
I'd like to be loved and feel the same.

There are many things I'd like to do,
And I'd like to do them all with you.
