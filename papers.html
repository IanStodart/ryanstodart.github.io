Child Language Learner

As an exercise for a Lingustic Anthropology class during my undergraduate program, I was tasked to observe a child between the ages of six and eighteen months and detail the child's language development stage. This includes a transcipt of speech into the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA). The paper details not only the child's speech patterns, but also looks at how his mother spoke to him, a factor that certainly impacts a child's speech development.

Research Grant Proposal: Monte Verde Peopling Site

An important skill for any academic professional is the knowledge of how to write a grant proposal. I wrote this one as an assignment for an archaeological field methods class during my undergraduate program. I chose to focus on an archaeological site in Chile called Monte Verde, which at the time was believed to have some of the earliest-dated evidence of humans in the Americas, particularly supporting the "Clovis First" theory of the peopling of the Americas. The paper, being a grant proposal, includeds travel and equipment expenses, details on housing arrangements, as well as a history on the site and an argument for additional research in the area.

Persistent Pestilence: The Plague

This is a paper I wrote for my medical anthroplogy class during my undergraduate program and details the historic and epidemiological aspects of the plague. It includes details on how the plague is transmitted, what the symptoms and progression of the disease entails, and how the disease impacted humans, both in the past and the present. This paper is not the most difficult or lengthy, but is one of my favorites.

Stem Cells: The Future of Medicine

Persuasive writing is a common topic for English classes throughout the education process; I co-wrote this persuasive paper for an English class during my undergraduate career focusing on stem cell medicine and the practical applications. We argued that stem cell research is vital for the future of medicine and has a multitude of applications, making it diverse and effective. We included some counterpoints as well as our rebuttals, positing that advancements in stem cell research and utility are overall benecial for everyone.

Religious Participant Observation: Buddhist Funeral

For a class on Magic, Shamanism, and Religion that I took during my undergradate career I wrote a paper describing my experience at a Buddhist funeral. This was also an exercise in participant-observation, which is a primary skill for any anthropologist. This paper details the process and traditions of the funeral I observed, as well as my perspective as an outsider to the Buddhist religion.

Max Gluckman Abstract

Max Gluckman was a social anthropologist from South Africa; Gluckman did a lot of work on, and combating, apartheid in Africa, particularly in South Africa and created the famous Manchester School in the University of Manchester, which heavily encouraged detailed case studies. He was a proponent of structural functionalism, which is a theory that states that every aspect of a society has some sort of purpose, hence "functional". He, and many of the other theorists, including Karl Marx, Emile Durkheim, and Franz Boas, was a fascinating individual who paved the way for the anthropology we know today.
